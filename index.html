QUIC Deployment in Vietnam

Insights from our 2025 nationwide QUIC scanning study

by Thanh Trung Nguyen, Thanh Le, Minh Hai Vu, Phi Le Nguyen, Kien Nguyen

View Interactive Map

QUIC [RFC 9000] is a network transport protocol originally developed by Google and standardized by the IETF in 2021. It aims to improve performance, reduce latency, and enhance security for Internet connections. As Vietnam's digital economy continues its rapid growth [e-Conomy SEA 2024], understanding the adoption of modern protocols like QUIC is crucial for optimizing network infrastructure.

This project presents the first large-scale analysis of QUIC protocol deployment across Vietnam. Using tools like ZMap [Durumeric et al., 2013], ZDNS, and QScanner [Zirngibl et al., 2024], we scanned over 16 million IPv4 addresses (sourced from VNNIC) to assess QUIC adoption and visualize its distribution geographically.

About this project

Researchers and network administrators often need accurate data on protocol adoption to evaluate the modernization of national internet infrastructure. However, comprehensive data on QUIC deployment in Vietnam has been scarce, limiting the ability to assess the readiness of local networks for next-generation transport protocols.

We uncovered how QUIC is being adopted across different ISPs and geographic regions in Vietnam. Our study provides a detailed snapshot of the current landscape, highlighting the dominance of IETF QUIC (RFC 9000) and the security configurations preferred by local servers. This data is crucial for understanding the performance and security implications of the shifting internet transport layer.

Overall, our project aims to provide transparency and actionable insights for the research community and network operators. To ensure reproducibility and foster further research, we openly share our methodology and findings. We provide an interactive visualization tool that allows users to explore the data granularly, improving the validity of our conclusions through better accessibility and awareness.
